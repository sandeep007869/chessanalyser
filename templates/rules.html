RULES

King
Moves one square in any direction (horizontal, vertical, diagonal). Must avoid moving into check. The game is lost if the king is checkmated.
Queen
Moves any number of squares in all directions. Combines the power of rook and bishop. Cannot jump over pieces.
Rook
Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically. Strong in open files and ranks. Also involved in castling.
Bishop
Moves diagonally across the board. Each bishop stays on one color (light or dark). Cannot jump over pieces
Knight
Moves in an L-shape (2 squares in one direction, then 1 sideways). Can jump over other pieces. Captures on the square it lands
Pawn
Moves forward one square (two squares from starting position). Captures diagonally, not forward. Promotes to another piece upon reaching the last rank.
Castling
Special move involving king and rook. King moves two squares toward rook; rook moves beside the king. Not allowed if king is in check or pieces have moved before.
En Passant
Special pawn capture. Occurs when an opponent’s pawn moves two squares forward from its starting position. Can be captured as if it moved only one square, but only on the next move.
